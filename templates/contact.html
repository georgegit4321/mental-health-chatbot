Understanding Mental Health

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Just like physical health, mental health is an essential component of overall wellness. Mental health conditions can affect anyone regardless of age, gender, geography, income, social status, race/ethnicity, religion/spirituality, sexual orientation, background, or other aspect of cultural identity.

Current Mental Health Challenges

Increasing Rates of Depression and Anxiety

Rates of depression and anxiety have risen significantly, particularly following global events like the COVID-19 pandemic.

Digital Mental Health

Social media and constant connectivity can contribute to feelings of isolation, comparison, and burnout.

Access to Mental Healthcare

Many communities lack adequate access to mental health services due to geographical, financial, or cultural barriers.

Stigma and Awareness

Despite progress, stigma around mental health issues remains a significant barrier to seeking help.

Self-Care Strategies

  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule
  • Engage in physical activity
  • Practice mindfulness or meditation
  • Connect with supportive friends and family
  • Limit exposure to news and social media
  • Seek professional help when needed
